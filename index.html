This active build log documents the full development history of TESSERACT TWIN — the Mumbai Real Estate Execution-Risk Digital Twin — from initial concept through the current production release. The project was developed as part of the EMSE 6099 Capstone at The George Washington University during Spring 2026 and continues as a proprietary commercial product.

A Financial and Project Management-Based Execution-Risk Framework for Mumbai Real Estate Projects

The framework models the complete 6-stage development lifecycle with zone-aware costing, FSI/DR/TDR strategy optimization, funding waterfall analysis, dynamic risk scoring, and side-by-side scenario comparison across Mumbai's nine micro-market zones.

Core Objective

Build a structured, repeatable decision-making framework for evaluating feasibility and execution risk of real estate development projects in Mumbai. The model serves three audiences: developers assessing project viability, lenders evaluating financing exposure, and academic committees evaluating methodological rigor.

04 · Phase 1: Problem Structuring & Research

January 13 – February 1, 2026

The project began with the spring semester on January 13. The first two weeks were spent defining the problem space, conducting a literature review on real estate project risk frameworks, and engaging our industry partner. I reached out to Webtech Realty in Mumbai to secure access to structured project data from their completed and ongoing developments, including The Presidential (a major commercial project).

The pre-proposal was submitted on January 17, followed by the full written proposal on January 27. The oral proposal presentation established the scope and methodology for the capstone committee.

Key Decision: Stage-Gated Architecture

Chose a 6-stage gate model (Feasibility, Land Lock-in, Regulatory, CC Approval, Construction, Completion) over a continuous timeline approach. This aligned with how developers, lenders, and regulators in Mumbai actually structure project progression. Every subsequent model version preserved this architecture.

06 · Phase 3: Excel Financial Engine Build

February 18 – March 5, 2026

This phase translated the architecture into a working financial model. Development moved fast: the core model went from blank spreadsheet to a 9-sheet, 358-formula execution risk framework in under 48 hours.

Preliminary Financial Model (Feb 24)

A 3-sheet prototype establishing baseline parameters: 250,000 sqft BUA, 180,000 sqft saleable area, project cost of 400 Cr, 12% cost of debt, 20% cost of equity. This validated that the cost-phasing logic worked before building the full stage-gated model.

Base Model v1 (Feb 25, 00:00)

Built overnight. Eight sheets implementing the complete stage-gate structure with a 60-month timeline. Each stage sheet tracked: opening capital position, stage-specific costs, equity/debt consumption, and closing position carried forward. A Dashboard sheet introduced the execution-risk scoring framework with per-stage success scores, ratings, and weights. An annotated reference copy was preserved with documentation columns marking INPUT vs LINK fields.

Execution Risk Model V1/V2 (Feb 25)

Expanded to 9 sheets by adding a centralized Project Setup sheet and a dedicated Cash Flow waterfall sheet. The V2 Reference Guide documented the full architecture: 358 formulas, 33 scored risk metrics, dynamic inter-stage capital chain with shortfall detection.

Market Dataset Compilation (Mar 1-10)

Created Mumbai_RE_Project_Dataset.xlsx with 8 sheets of structured market intelligence: 54 projects (26 attributes each) from MahaRERA/Knight Frank/Anarock/CBRE/JLL, area-wise benchmarks for 9 zones, cost component benchmarks, delay analysis (27 cause categories), timeline benchmarks by project type, Ready Reckoner rates FY 2025-26, and market statistics from 2019-2024.

07 · Phase 4: The Pivot to Interactive Dashboard

March 3 – March 18, 2026

This was the pivotal phase where the project shifted from a static Excel model to a dynamic browser-based dashboard. The decision was driven by a simple realization: stakeholder presentations required "what-if" analysis in real time, and Excel could not deliver the interactivity needed.

Why the Platform Pivot?

An interactive dashboard offers real-time recalculation on parameter changes, instant zone switching, visual feedback through charts and maps, and zero-dependency distribution (any browser, any OS). For a risk model that needs scenario exploration, the browser platform was clearly superior to a spreadsheet.

V3: First HTML Dashboard (Mar 5, 690 lines)

Proof of concept. Single-file HTML/CSS/JS with Chart.js v4.5.1. Basic KPI cards, 6-stage cost breakdown in a card grid, interactive scenario panel with a recalculate button, and 5 Chart.js visualizations. The key validation: the entire financial model ran correctly in the browser, matching Excel outputs.

V4: Zone Selection and FSI Calculator (Mar 13, 900 lines)

First major feature expansion. Added a zone selector with 9 interactive cards, FSI calculator panel, alert banners for shortfall warnings, Gantt-style timeline visualization, health scoring table with status badges, and sensitivity tornado chart. Chart.js settled on v4.4.1 which remained through all subsequent versions.

FSI/DR/TDR Research (Mar 14-18)

Created the FSI/DR/TDR Purchase Analysis document after consulting with industry professionals. This research directly informed the DR strategy module added in V5.3. It covered development rights acquisition frameworks, TDR market rates by locality, BMC premium payment scheduling, and four acquisition strategies.

The dashboard detects API availability at startup. If the backend is unreachable, it falls back seamlessly to hardcoded data arrays. This means the tool always works: on an airplane with just the HTML file, from a USB drive, or connected to a shared team server.

11 · Phase 8: Compare Mode & Twin Engine

April 3 – Present (Active Development)

This phase introduced the most architecturally significant feature since the platform pivot: fully independent side-by-side scenario comparison. The "Twin" in Digital Twin became literal — users can now run two complete, independent sessions simultaneously and compare execution risk across different zone/parameter configurations in real time.

Architecture Decision: Iframe Isolation

The core challenge was that TESSERACT's financial engine uses ~30+ global variables (selectedZone, charts, lastCalc, fsiPieChartInstance, etc.) tightly coupled through recalculate(). Refactoring all of this for multi-instance support would have required rewriting ~2,500 lines. Instead, each compare session runs in its own <iframe>, providing complete state isolation at zero refactoring cost. Communication between parent and iframes uses the postMessage API.

V5.6.3: Compare Mode Foundation (Apr 3, ~2,700 lines)

First implementation of dual-session Compare Mode. Added a Compare button in the header, full-screen overlay with two iframe panes (Session A and Session B), each running an independent instance of the dashboard via ?embedded=1&session=A|B query parameters. Embedded mode hides the header and user profile tab inside iframes, adds session accent bars (blue for A, green for B), and syncs dark mode via postMessage.

V5.7: Enhanced Compare UI (Apr 3, ~2,760 lines)

Major UI refinement for compare mode. Added a branded TESSERACT TWIN header to the compare overlay with the SVG logo, consistent typography, and a single user profile mini-avatar. Universal dark mode toggle in the compare header syncs to both iframes simultaneously. Session labels with color-coded accent lines (Session A: blue, Session B: green) for instant visual differentiation.

V5.7.1: Session Persistence & Polish (Apr 3, ~2,800 lines)

Four targeted fixes in a single version:

V5.7.2: Score Gauges & Navigation Fix (Apr 4, ~2,840 lines)

Added real-time composite score gauges in the compare header — each session displays its overall risk score with color-coded circular indicators and rating labels. Scores update live as users modify parameters in either iframe.

postMessage vs contentDocument

The initial implementation polled iframe DOMs via contentDocument to read scores. This works on http:// but browsers block cross-origin DOM access on file:// protocol for security. Fix: switched to postMessage — each iframe's updateCompositeGauge() posts {type:'scoreUpdate', session, score, color, rating} to the parent, and the parent listens for these messages. This works universally regardless of protocol.

Also added "Sample" test buttons per session in the compare header, and fixed navigation so clicking "Overview" while compare sessions are running re-opens the compare overlay instead of navigating to the regular overview page. Added compareClosingIntentional flag to prevent the exit button from getting trapped in a reopen loop.

16 · Lessons Learned

Architecture: Simplicity Wins

The 6-stage gate structure was chosen on day one and never changed. The V5.0 experiment with 10 finer-grained phases confirmed a counterintuitive principle: more granularity does not always produce better insight. Building the entire dashboard as a single HTML file was unconventional but practical: it can be opened in any browser, shared as an email attachment, embedded in Electron, or served from a static host with zero dependency management.

Iframe Isolation Over Refactoring

When Compare Mode required running two independent sessions, the instinct was to refactor all ~30 global variables into an instance-based architecture. Instead, we chose iframe isolation: each session runs in its own iframe with complete state separation. The cost was postMessage-based IPC for dark mode sync, score updates, and sample triggers. The benefit was zero risk of breaking recalculate() or any of the ~470 lines of financial engine logic. Sometimes the pragmatic architecture beats the elegant one.

Dual-Track Validation

Maintaining both Excel and HTML versions throughout provided a critical cross-validation mechanism. When the HTML dashboard produced a different result from the Excel model, it always pointed to a bug in one or the other.

The Cost of Not Testing

The V5.5 broken build was a turning point. The 574-check test suite for V5.6.1 paid for itself immediately when V5.6.2 code cleanup needed regression verification.

Protocol Matters

The score gauge bug in V5.7.2 taught a valuable lesson: contentDocument access works fine on http:// but is blocked on file:// due to browser security policies. The fix (postMessage) works universally. Always test on the actual deployment protocol, not just localhost.

Domain Expertise Shapes Software

The FSI/DR/TDR module illustrates how industry knowledge transforms a model. Conversations with Mumbai development professionals revealed the complexity of BMC premium financing, TDR market dynamics, and regulatory nuances that no amount of pure engineering could have anticipated.
